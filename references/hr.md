# HR — CEO Guide

Use this when the CEO needs to act on or communicate about: hiring decisions, performance reviews, team updates, org changes, compensation, terminations, culture issues, or headcount planning.

---

## Standard output format

**HR Summary — [Period / Topic]**

**Headline:** [One sentence on the most important HR development]

**Headcount snapshot:**
| | This Month | Last Month | Change |
|--|--|--|--|
| Total Employees | X | X | +/- |
| Open Roles | X | X | +/- |
| Attrition (resigned/terminated) | X | X | +/- |

**Highlights:**
- [Key hire, promotion, or team win]
- [Notable departure or risk]

**Issues requiring CEO attention:** [Be direct — what needs a decision or action now?]

**Recommended action:** [One clear recommendation]

**Next steps:**
1. [Owner] — [Action] — [By when]

---

## How to handle specific HR situations

### Hiring decision
Present it as:
- **Role:** [Title, team, why it's needed]
- **Finalist(s):** [Name(s), one-line summary of fit]
- **Recommendation:** Hire [Name] because [one reason]. Start date target: [date].
- **Risk if we don't hire:** [What breaks or slows down]

### Performance review summary
- Flag employees in 3 buckets only: **Strong** / **Needs improvement** / **At risk**
- For "at risk" employees: what's the issue, what's been tried, what's the recommended next step (PIP, manager coaching, exit)
- Never list every employee — surface only the ones that need CEO awareness

### Team announcement draft
Use this structure:
> [What's changing] + [Why we made this decision] + [What it means for you] + [What happens next]

Keep it under 200 words. No jargon. No corporate speak.

### Attrition / resignation
- Who left, what role, how long they were with the company
- Was this voluntary or involuntary?
- Impact on the team (low / medium / high)
- Replacement plan: backfill immediately, redistribute work, or hold for now?
- If pattern: is this the 2nd or 3rd departure from same team? Flag it.

### Compensation / salary review
- What is the proposed change?
- Is it market-rate? (above / at / below benchmark)
- Budget impact per year
- CEO recommendation: approve, negotiate, or defer

### Org change / restructure
- What's changing in the reporting structure?
- Who is affected?
- Why now? (growth, underperformance, strategic shift)
- How will it be communicated and when?

---

## Red flags to always surface

- Attrition rate above 15% annually — especially if it's concentrated in one team
- A key role open for more than 60 days
- Multiple people leaving the same manager
- Compensation significantly below market in a competitive role
- A performance issue that hasn't been formally documented

---

## CEO communication tone for HR matters

- **Sensitive situations** (terminations, PIPs, mental health): private, empathetic, clear
- **Org changes**: transparent about the reason, focused on stability
- **Hiring announcements**: enthusiastic but professional — why this person, why now
- **Team performance wins**: specific praise, not generic ("The sales team closed $800K in Q2" not "everyone did great")

Never share individual performance details in group communications. Keep sensitive HR matters between the CEO, HR, and direct managers only.
