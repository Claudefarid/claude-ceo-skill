# Hiring Playbook — CEO Guide

Use this when the CEO needs to open a role, structure an interview process, evaluate a candidate, make an offer, negotiate compensation, or onboard a new executive or senior hire.

---

## Standard output format

**Hiring Summary — [Role]**

**Why we're hiring:** [Business need in one sentence]
**Urgency:** Immediate / This quarter / Planning ahead
**Type:** Backfill / New role / Upgrade

**Recommended next step:** [One action the CEO should take today]

---

## Opening a new role

### Before writing the job description, answer these:
1. What specific outcome does this person need to achieve in their first 90 days?
2. What would make this hire a failure at 6 months?
3. Is this a backfill (replace someone), upgrade (hire better than before), or new capability?
4. What is the budget? (salary range, equity, timeline to hire)
5. Is this truly a hire — or could it be a contractor, fractional exec, or agency first?

### Job description structure (for CEO review)
```
ROLE: [Title]
TEAM: [Reports to / department]
LOCATION: [Remote / hybrid / onsite]

THE OPPORTUNITY
[2–3 sentences: what this person will build or own, why it matters now]

WHAT YOU'LL DO (first 90 days)
- [Specific outcome 1]
- [Specific outcome 2]
- [Specific outcome 3]

WHAT WE'RE LOOKING FOR
Must have:
- [Non-negotiable requirement]
- [Non-negotiable requirement]
Nice to have:
- [Preferred but flexible]

COMPENSATION
- Salary: $X–$X
- Equity: X%–X% (vesting over 4 years, 1-year cliff)
- Benefits: [list]
```

**Rule:** If you can't write 3 specific 90-day outcomes, you're not ready to hire. Figure out the role first.

## Interview process design

### For senior / executive hires (VP, C-suite)
Use a 4-stage process. Total time: 2–3 weeks max.

**Stage 1 — Recruiter / HR screen (30 min)**
- Role fit, comp alignment, logistics
- Pass/fail: basic alignment confirmed

**Stage 2 — CEO or hiring manager (45–60 min)**
- Explore career narrative: what drove each major move?
- Past results: "Walk me through the most important thing you built or fixed in your last role — what was the outcome in numbers?"
- Culture and values: how they make decisions, how they handle failure

**Stage 3 — Work sample or case (2–4 hrs candidate time)**
- Give a realistic problem they'd face in the role
- Example for Head of Sales: "Here's our pipeline data and ICP. Build the first 90-day plan."
- Example for CMO: "Here's our funnel. Where are the gaps and what would you prioritize?"
- Assess: thinking process, not just output

**Stage 4 — Panel (90 min)**
- Meet 2–3 peers or direct reports
- Give each interviewer a specific dimension to assess (not the same questions)
- Debrief together immediately after

### Interview questions by dimension

**Results and execution**
- "What's the biggest outcome you've driven in the last 2 years — in numbers?"
- "Tell me about a goal you missed. What happened and what did you do?"
- "Walk me through how you'd spend your first 30 days in this role."

**Leadership and people**
- "How do you handle a direct report who is underperforming?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news to your team."
- "Who is the best person you've ever hired? Why? Could we talk to them?"

**Strategic thinking**
- "What's something most people in your field believe that you think is wrong?"
- "Where do you see [our industry] in 3 years?"
- "What would you do in this role that I haven't thought of yet?"

**Culture and values**
- "Tell me about a decision you made that others disagreed with. What happened?"
- "What environment brings out your best work?"
- "What would your last manager say is your biggest development area?"

## Compensation benchmarks

### Salary ranges by stage (US market, adjust for country)

| Role | Early Stage (<$2M ARR) | Growth ($2M–$10M ARR) | Scale ($10M+ ARR) |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| VP Sales | $120–160K | $160–220K | $220–300K |
| VP Marketing | $110–150K | $150–200K | $200–280K |
| VP Engineering | $140–180K | $180–250K | $250–350K |
| CFO | $120–160K | $160–220K | $220–320K |
| COO | $130–170K | $170–230K | $230–320K |
| Head of Product | $120–160K | $160–210K | $210–280K |
| Head of CS | $100–130K | $130–170K | $170–220K |

*These are base salary ranges. Add 20–30% for total cash (bonus). Equity is separate.*

### Equity guidelines (startup, pre-IPO)

| Role | Seed stage | Series A | Series B+ |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| C-suite (new) | 0.5–2.0% | 0.25–0.75% | 0.1–0.3% |
| VP / Director | 0.1–0.5% | 0.05–0.2% | 0.025–0.1% |
| Senior IC | 0.05–0.15% | 0.025–0.1% | 0.01–0.05% |

Standard vesting: 4 years, 1-year cliff. Always use 409A valuation for strike price.

## Offer negotiation

When a candidate counters:
- Ask first: "Is it the base, equity, or total package?" — know what they're negotiating before responding
- Never go to your max immediately — leave room for a counter
- If you can't match base: offer more equity, accelerated vesting, signing bonus, or earlier first review
- If they're still not moving: "Help me understand — is it purely the number, or is there something else?"
- Know your walk-away point before the call — don't decide under pressure

## Red flags in candidates

- Can't give specific numbers for past results ("we grew a lot" vs "we grew 3x to $5M ARR")
- References only come from people they manage, not people they reported to
- Blames external factors for every failure
- Has never stayed at a company more than 18 months without a clear reason
- Can't explain what they'd do differently if they could redo something
- Asks about work hours and vacation before asking about the role or mission

## 90-day onboarding plan for senior hires

### Days 1–30: Listen and learn
- 1:1 with every key stakeholder
- Review all relevant data (metrics, strategy docs, roadmap)
- No major decisions yet — understand first
- Weekly check-in with CEO

### Days 31–60: Assess and plan
- Present initial findings and observations to CEO
- Identify quick wins (things they can improve in 30 days)
- Identify the 1–2 structural changes needed
- Build relationships with direct reports

### Days 61–90: Execute and commit
- Deliver on 2–3 quick wins
- Present 6-month plan to CEO and team
- Own their domain — CEO steps back
- Formal 90-day review with CEO

**CEO rule:** If a senior hire hasn't shown clear signs of ownership and impact by day 90, have the honest conversation at day 90 — not day 180.

---

## Hiring red flags at the company level

- Time-to-hire exceeding 60 days for critical roles
- Same role re-opened within 12 months (mis-hire or poor onboarding)
- Offer acceptance rate below 70% (compensation or culture perception issue)
- No structured interview process — hiring on gut feel
- CEO involved in every hire below VP level (not scalable)

## Live data — Google Sheets + Slack integration

### Google Sheets — headcount planning and pipeline tracking
Many companies track their hiring pipeline in a spreadsheet. If shared:
```
- Current open roles (title, department, days open, hiring manager)
- Candidates in pipeline by stage (applied → screen → interview → offer → hired)
- Offer acceptance rate (offers extended vs offers accepted)
- Time-to-hire by role and department
- Headcount plan vs actuals (planned hires vs actual hires this quarter)
- Budget remaining for hiring (if tracked)
```

**Tell the CEO to share their hiring tracker:**
> "Share your hiring or ATS tracker sheet with the CEO Assistant service account. I'll read it automatically and flag roles that have been open too long or pipelines that are too thin."

**Red flags from hiring sheet:**
- Role open > 45 days with fewer than 5 qualified candidates → sourcing problem
- Offer acceptance rate < 70% → compensation or culture perception issue
- Multiple roles open in same team → capacity crisis or growth bottleneck
- Time-to-hire > 60 days for senior roles → process too slow, losing candidates

### Slack — hiring signals
When Slack is connected:
```
- Check #hiring or #recruiting channel for active discussions
- Look for messages about candidate feedback from interview panels
- Find any team concerns about understaffing raised in team channels
```
