# Summarizing Reports & Data

Use this guide when the CEO wants to understand a document, dataset, spreadsheet, meeting notes, or any long piece of content quickly.

## The core job

Turn complexity into clarity. The CEO does not need to read everything — they need to know:
1. What happened or what this is about (one sentence)
2. Why it matters to the business (one sentence)
3. What they should do about it (one sentence)

Everything else is supporting detail.

## Summary structure

Use this format for any summary:

---

**[TOPIC] — Executive Summary**

**The headline:** [Single most important takeaway in one sentence]

**Why it matters:** [Business impact — revenue, risk, timing, people]

**Key details:**
- [Most important data point or finding]
- [Second most important]
- [Third, only if truly needed]

**Recommended action:** [What the CEO should do next, and by when]

---

## Rules

- Lead with the insight, not the background. Don't explain what the report is before telling the CEO what it says.
- Use numbers when available. "Revenue grew" is weak. "Revenue grew 18% MoM to $420K" is useful.
- Apply the "So what?" test to every sentence. If removing it changes nothing, cut it.
- Max length: ~400 words unless the CEO asks for more.
- If the document has a recommendation buried at the end, surface it at the top.

## Common inputs and how to handle them

**Financial report:** Lead with the most important metric (revenue, burn, margin). Compare to last period. Flag any unexpected change. One-line interpretation.

**Market research / competitor report:** What changed, and what does it mean for our strategy? No need to relay every finding — just the ones that affect decisions.

**Meeting notes:** What was decided? What are the action items and who owns them? What's still unresolved?

**Long email chain:** What is the sender actually asking for? What's the history in one sentence? What should the CEO reply?

**Survey or feedback data:** What is the single clearest signal from the data? What should change as a result?

## When you don't have the document

If the CEO says "summarize this" but hasn't attached a document, ask for it directly:

> "Please share the document or paste the content and I'll turn it into a clean summary."
