# Finance Reports — CEO Guide

Use this when the CEO needs to understand or act on financial reports: P&L, cash flow, budget vs actuals, burn rate, revenue breakdowns, or financial forecasts.

---

## Standard output format

**Finance Summary — [Period]**

**Bottom line:** [One sentence: is the business healthy, under pressure, or at risk?]

**Key numbers:**
| Metric | This Period | Last Period | Change |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Revenue | $X | $X | +/-% |
| Gross Profit | $X | $X | +/-% |
| Net Profit / Loss | $X | $X | +/-% |
| Cash on Hand | $X | — | — |
| Burn Rate | $X/mo | $X/mo | +/-% |
| Runway | X months | — | — |

**What's working:** [1–2 sentences on the strongest financial signal]

**What needs attention:** [1–2 sentences on the biggest concern]

**Recommended action:** [What the CEO should do — not a list of options, a decision]

**Next steps:**
1. [Owner] — [Action] — [By when]
2. [Owner] — [Action] — [By when]

## How to read common financial reports

### P&L (Profit & Loss)
- **Revenue** = money coming in
- **COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)** = direct cost to deliver the product/service
- **Gross Profit** = Revenue minus COGS — shows core business efficiency
- **Operating Expenses** = salaries, rent, software, marketing — what it costs to run the company
- **Net Profit / Net Loss** = what's left after everything — the bottom line
- **EBITDA** = Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization — a measure of operational profitability

**CEO question to ask:** Is gross margin improving? If revenue is growing but gross profit is shrinking, that's a problem.

### Cash Flow Statement
- **Operating Cash Flow** = cash generated by the core business
- **Investing Cash Flow** = money spent on assets (equipment, acquisitions)
- **Financing Cash Flow** = money from loans, investors, or repayments
- **Net Cash Position** = total cash left at end of period

**CEO question to ask:** Is operating cash flow positive? A business can show profit on paper but still run out of cash.

### Budget vs Actuals
- Shows what was planned vs what actually happened
- **Favorable variance** = spent less than planned or earned more than planned
- **Unfavorable variance** = over budget or under target

**CEO question to ask:** Where are the biggest variances and why? An overspend in one area is fine if it's driving growth.

### Burn Rate & Runway
- **Burn rate** = how much cash the company spends each month (net)
- **Runway** = how many months until cash runs out at current burn rate
- **Formula:** Runway = Cash on Hand ÷ Monthly Net Burn

**CEO rule of thumb:** If runway is under 12 months, it's time to act — raise, cut, or accelerate revenue.

---

## Red flags to flag immediately

- Revenue declining 2+ months in a row
- Gross margin below 40% (SaaS), 20% (services), or dropping month-over-month
- Cash runway under 6 months with no funding in sight
- Accounts receivable (money owed to you) growing faster than revenue
- A single customer representing more than 30% of revenue

---

## When the CEO gets a finance report

1. Find the 3 most important numbers
2. Compare them to last period
3. Flag any number that moved more than 10% in either direction
4. State the implication in one sentence
5. Give a recommendation

Do not relay every line item. Summarize and highlight.

## Live data — QuickBooks integration

When QuickBooks MCP is connected, pull data automatically before presenting any finance summary.

**What to pull:**
```
- P&L for current month and last month (for MoM comparison)
- Balance sheet (cash on hand, accounts receivable)
- Cash flow statement (operating cash flow)
- Expense breakdown by category (top 5 expense lines)
- Revenue by customer (top 10, to check concentration)
```

**After pulling, auto-fill these fields in company-profile.md:**
- `cash_on_hand` — from balance sheet
- `burn_rate` — from total operating expenses (monthly)
- `gross_margin` — from P&L (gross profit ÷ revenue)
- `runway_months` — cash on hand ÷ burn rate
- `mrr_or_arr` — from revenue line

**If QuickBooks is not connected:**
> "I can analyze your finances in detail if you paste your P&L or export from QuickBooks. Alternatively, say 'how do I connect QuickBooks' and I'll walk you through it."

**Red flags to check from QuickBooks data:**
- Accounts receivable > 60 days overdue → customers are slow to pay
- Any single expense category growing >30% MoM without corresponding revenue growth
- Cash balance declining faster than the stated burn rate
- Revenue recognized ≠ cash collected (accrual vs cash timing issue)
