# Meetings — CEO Guide

Use this when the CEO needs to prepare for an important meeting, capture action items after one, or structure a meeting agenda that drives a decision.

---

## Pre-meeting brief format

**Meeting Brief — [Meeting Name] — [Date, Time]**

**Attendees:** [Names and roles — one line each]

**CEO's goal for this meeting:** [What outcome do you need? Decision / relationship / information / commitment]

**Context in 3 sentences:** [What's the situation, what led to this meeting, what's at stake]

**The 2–3 things the CEO must say:**
1. [Key point or position]
2. [Key point or position]
3. [Key point or position, if needed]

**Likely objections or hard questions:**
- [Question] → [Suggested response]
- [Question] → [Suggested response]

**Desired outcome:** [What should be agreed, decided, or committed to before leaving the room]

**If the meeting goes off track:** [What's the fallback — reschedule, table the topic, or escalate]

---

## Post-meeting action items format

**Meeting Summary — [Meeting Name] — [Date]**

**Decision made:** [What was agreed — one sentence]

**Action items:**
| # | Action | Owner | Due Date |
|---|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 | [Specific task] | [Name] | [Date] |
| 2 | [Specific task] | [Name] | [Date] |
| 3 | [Specific task] | [Name] | [Date] |

**What was left unresolved:** [Anything that needs a follow-up meeting or decision]

**Follow-up communication needed?** Yes / No — [If yes, who sends what to whom by when]

## Meeting types and how to handle each

### Board meeting prep
- Load `references/investor.md` for the board package structure
- Pre-align with key board members 48 hours before on anything sensitive
- Have data ready to back every claim
- Know the 1–2 decisions you need the board to make
- Prepare for the questions that make you uncomfortable — those will come up

### Investor / fundraising meeting
- Open with traction (not the problem statement — they've heard it before)
- Have a tight 3-sentence answer to: "Why now?" and "Why you?"
- Know your numbers cold: ARR, growth rate, burn, runway, LTV:CAC
- End with the ask: amount, timeline, what it achieves

### Sales / partnership meeting (CEO attending)
- Why is the CEO in this meeting? (close the deal, elevate the relationship, unstick progress)
- What has already been discussed? (don't ask what was covered in previous calls)
- What does the other party want from this meeting?
- CEO's role: relationship and vision — not discovery questions

### All-hands / team meeting
- Open with a business update (honest, specific numbers)
- Acknowledge the team's work with specific examples
- Share one strategic priority for the next period
- Leave time for real Q&A — not just "any questions?"
- Close with energy and clarity about what's next

### 1:1 with a direct report
- Ask: what's going well, what's stuck, and what do you need from me?
- Give: one piece of specific feedback (positive or constructive)
- Agree: one thing each person will do before next 1:1
- Note any follow-ups that need action

### Difficult conversation (performance, conflict, bad news)
- Prepare the key message in one sentence before the meeting
- Lead with the fact, not the feeling ("We're letting you go" not "This is really hard...")
- Be direct, brief, and private
- Have HR present for formal situations
- Follow up in writing for any formal action taken

## CEO meeting principles

1. **Every meeting needs a goal.** If you can't state what decision or outcome the meeting produces, don't hold it.
2. **Prepare the uncomfortable questions.** Think of the 3 questions you hope no one asks — and prepare answers for all of them.
3. **Close every meeting with action items.** Verbal agreements disappear. Named owners with dates stick.
4. **Don't let silence mean agreement.** Before ending, ask: "Is there anything we haven't said that needs to be said?"
5. **The CEO's job in most meetings is to decide, not to discuss.** When the team presents options, your job is to choose — not to add more options.
