# Industry Benchmarks — CEO Reference

Use this file to contextualize any metric the CEO shares. When presenting numbers, always show the benchmark alongside the actual.

Format: **"Your number: X — Benchmark for [stage/type]: Y — Status: ✅ / ⚠️ / 🔴"**

---

## How to use benchmarks

1. Check the CEO's company stage from `company-profile.md`
2. Check the company type (SaaS, services, ecommerce, marketplace)
3. Find the relevant benchmark below
4. Present it inline when showing metrics — never make the CEO ask "is this good?"

---

## SaaS Benchmarks

### Revenue Growth
| Stage | Good | Watch | Danger |
|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Early ($0–$500K ARR) | >15% MoM | 5–15% MoM | <5% MoM |
| Growth ($500K–$5M ARR) | >10% MoM | 3–10% MoM | <3% MoM |
| Scale ($5M+ ARR) | >5% MoM | 1–5% MoM | <1% MoM |

### Gross Margin
| Type | Good | Watch | Danger |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| SaaS / Software | >70% | 50–70% | <50% |
| SaaS + Services | >50% | 35–50% | <35% |
| Marketplace | >60% | 40–60% | <40% |

### Churn Rate (Monthly)
| Stage | Good | Watch | Danger |
|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Early | <3% | 3–5% | >5% |
| Growth | <2% | 2–4% | >4% |
| Scale | <1% | 1–2% | >2% |

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
| Level | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| >120% | Excellent — customers are expanding faster than churning |
| 100–120% | Good — growing from existing base |
| 90–100% | Watch — barely replacing churn |
| <90% | Danger — shrinking from existing customers |

### CAC Payback Period
| Stage | Good | Watch | Danger |
|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Early | <18 months | 18–24 months | >24 months |
| Growth | <12 months | 12–18 months | >18 months |
| Scale | <6 months | 6–12 months | >12 months |

### LTV:CAC Ratio
| Ratio | Status |
|-------|--------|
| >5:1 | Excellent — underspending on growth |
| 3:1–5:1 | Good — healthy unit economics |
| 1:1–3:1 | Watch — marginal or unsustainable |
| <1:1 | Danger — losing money on every customer |

### Sales Quota Attainment
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| >80% team hitting quota | Excellent |
| 60–80% hitting quota | Good |
| 40–60% hitting quota | Watch |
| <40% hitting quota | Danger |

### Magic Number (Sales Efficiency)
| Score | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| >1.0 | Excellent — very efficient growth |
| 0.75–1.0 | Good — spend more on sales/marketing |
| 0.5–0.75 | Watch — efficiency improving needed |
| <0.5 | Danger — growth is too expensive |

### Rule of 40
Revenue growth rate % + Profit margin % should be ≥ 40

| Score | Status |
|-------|--------|
| >40 | Healthy business |
| 20–40 | Acceptable, room to improve |
| <20 | Under pressure |

## Universal Benchmarks (all company types)

### Runway
| Months | Status |
|--------|--------|
| >18 months | Safe |
| 12–18 months | Start planning next raise or path to profitability |
| 6–12 months | Urgent — fundraise or cut now |
| <6 months | Critical — immediate action required |

### Employee Attrition (Annual)
| Level | Status |
|-------|--------|
| <10% | Low — healthy retention |
| 10–20% | Normal — monitor by department |
| >20% | High — investigate root cause |
| >30% | Crisis — systemic problem |

### NPS Score
| Score | Status |
|-------|--------|
| >70 | World class |
| 50–70 | Excellent |
| 30–50 | Good |
| 0–30 | Needs improvement |
| <0 | Danger — more detractors than promoters |

### Support Response Time (B2B SaaS)
| Tier | Good | Watch | Danger |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| Critical | <1 hr | 1–4 hrs | >4 hrs |
| High | <4 hrs | 4–8 hrs | >8 hrs |
| Normal | <24 hrs | 24–48 hrs | >48 hrs |

## How to present benchmarks in responses

Always use this format when showing a metric:

> **MRR: $85,000** ↑ 12% MoM
> Benchmark (Growth stage SaaS): 10% MoM is good. You're above — strong signal.

> **Churn: 4.2%** ↑ from 3.8% last month
> Benchmark (Growth stage SaaS): Good is <2%, Watch is 2–4%. You're in the danger zone — this needs immediate attention.

Never just show the number. Always show the benchmark and the interpretation.
